                                                        Additional Details
                                                          If you do not have a disability please select "No disability" from the drop down menu.

                                                          Anglia Ruskin University welcomes students with disabilities and those who need additional learning support. We try to meet your needs wherever we can. Any information you give us on this form will not affect your application but it will help us to make any arrangements required to support your individual needs and your success in completing your course at our university.

                                                          If you’re a UK/EU student, a Study Support Adviser will contact you if you're made an offer. If you’re an International student we will send you some information and ask you to provide further details. A Study Support Adviser will then advise you on how we can support you.

                                                          Please visit the Study Support Section of our website for more information.

                                                            A criminal conviction will not necessarily prevent you from studying at our university. However we have a responsibility to ensure we provide a safe environment for students and visitors. Further information about our Criminal Convictions procedure can be found on our website.

                                                            Students on courses which include interaction with children or vulnerable adults will be required to obtain a satisfactory Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check. Please visit the DBS checks area of our website for more information.
